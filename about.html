Colegio De Montalban History

Colegio De Montalban is a government-funded university in Kasiglahan Village, Rodriguez, Rizal, Philippines. It was established on September 25, 2003 by virtue of Municipal Ordinance No. 03-24, and approved by the Sangguniang Bayan ng Rodriguez. On the following year, a couple of ordinances were approved by the council to fund and operate the college. On November 5, 2004, Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA) granted the college the authority to offer the following two-year courses: Nursing Aide, Civil Technology, Computer Technology, and Garments Technology.


From July 2004 to July 2010, CdM was under the administration of Mayor Pedro S. Cuerpo, who was subsequently succeeded by Mayor Cecilio C. Hernandez since July 2010. On July 7, 2014, by virtue of Sangguniang Bayan ng Rodriguez Ordinance No. 14-15, Pamantasan ng Montalban was renamed Colegio de Montalban.

  • November 5, 2004: TESDA approved PnM to offer four two-year courses: Nursing Aide, Civil Technology, Computer Technology, and Garments Technology.
  • April 4, 2005: Municipal Ordinance No. 05-07 officially established PnM and allocated funds for its operations.
  • May-June 2005: CHED granted permits to offer Bachelor's degrees, including BS Entrepreneurial Management, BS Commerce (Management), and BEEd with Early Childhood Education.
  • 2006-2007: CHED authorized the second- and third-year levels of several programs, including Civil Engineering, Computer Engineering, BEEd, and BS Entrepreneurship.
  • 2008-2009: CHED issued Certificates of Compliance for higher levels of programs like BSIT, BS Civil Engineering, and BS Entrepreneurship. Recognition was also granted for Teacher Education Programs and Technology-based courses.
  • 2011: CHED granted full government recognition to several programs, including BEEd, BSEd (majors in Biological Science and TLE), BSIT, BSCPE, BSBA-HRDM, and BS Entrepreneurship.
  • July 7, 2014: PnM was renamed Colegio de Montalban through Ordinance No. 14-15, confirmed by Sangguniang Panlalawigan ng Rizal in October 2014.
  • Present: Colegio de Montalban continues to provide various programs and aims to foster the holistic development of its students.
